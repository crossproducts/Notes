# Lumen House Brand Standards Handbook

## 1. Brand Foundations

Lumen House exists to provide understated, thoughtful hospitality. We do not compete on spectacle, extravagance, or trend-driven luxury. Our guests choose Lumen House for calm, care, and considered detail.

### 1.1 Our Definition of Luxury

At Lumen House, luxury means:

- Precision in service
- Quality without ostentation
- Calm environments
- Thoughtful personalisation

Luxury at Lumen House must never be described as “extravagant,” “opulent,” or “ultra-luxury.” These terms are inconsistent with our positioning.

### 1.2 Language Principles

All public-facing communication must be:

- Measured
- Clear
- Confident but never boastful
- Warm without being informal

We avoid exaggerated claims and superlatives unless they are objectively verifiable.

For example, phrases such as:
- “Best hotel in the city”
- “Unmatched experience”
- “World-famous service”

are prohibited unless supported by a current, verifiable award from a recognised authority.

---

## 2. Guest Communication Standards

### 2.1 Tone of Voice

Written and spoken communication must reflect quiet confidence. Staff should:

- Use complete sentences
- Avoid slang
- Avoid over-familiar language
- Avoid excessive enthusiasm (e.g., multiple exclamation marks)

The tone should feel attentive and composed.

### 2.2 Required Practices

When responding to guest concerns:

- Acknowledge the issue clearly.
- Take responsibility where appropriate.
- Avoid defensive language.
- Offer a clear next step.

It is required that all written responses to formal complaints include:
- A direct acknowledgment of the issue.
- A clear statement of what will happen next.

### 2.3 Discretion

Guest privacy is paramount.

Staff must not reference other guests in communication, even in anonymised or anecdotal form.

---

## 3. Room & Property Standards

### 3.1 Mandatory In-Room Features

All Lumen House rooms must include:

- High-quality cotton linens
- A locally sourced welcome item
- A handwritten or printed welcome note
- Refillable water in glass bottles

These are mandatory across all properties.

### 3.2 Optional Amenities

Some amenities may vary by property location and building constraints. These include:

- In-room coffee machines
- Bathtubs
- Balcony access

Properties must not imply that optional amenities are universal unless they are present in all locations.

### 3.3 Sustainability Commitment

Lumen House is committed to responsible hospitality.

Properties must:

- Eliminate single-use plastic toiletries.
- Offer guests the option to decline daily linen changes.
- Source welcome items from local suppliers where feasible.

Marketing materials must not describe the brand as “zero impact” or “carbon neutral” unless formally certified.

---

## 4. Marketing & Public Representation

### 4.1 Brand Claims

All claims made in marketing materials must be accurate and verifiable.

The term “luxury” may be used sparingly and without intensifiers. Language that exaggerates status, scale, or exclusivity beyond what can be objectively supported is not permitted.

Marketing copy must not rely on inflated positioning or competitive comparisons that cannot be substantiated.

### 4.2 Partnerships

Lumen House may collaborate with external brands whose values align with craftsmanship, responsible production, durability, and thoughtful design.

Partnerships must not create tension with our commitments to sustainability, long-term value, and considered consumption.

Before announcing any collaboration publicly, teams should assess whether the partner’s reputation, production practices, and brand positioning align with Lumen House’s standards of care and responsibility.

### 4.3 Imagery & Atmosphere

Imagery used in marketing should reflect:

- Natural light
- Real spaces
- Authentic guest experiences

Heavily staged or artificial imagery that exaggerates room size or atmosphere is not permitted.

---

## 5. Handling Ambiguity

Not every situation will be explicitly addressed in this handbook.

When guidance is unclear:

- Default to restraint rather than exaggeration.
- Prioritise guest comfort and dignity.
- Escalate to the General Manager if reputational risk is possible.

Staff should not invent standards or policies not described in this document.

---

## 6. Illustrative Scenarios (Non-Binding Guidance)

The following examples are provided to illustrate brand principles. They are not additional policy requirements.

### 6.1 Example: Upselling a Suite

Instead of:
“This is our most luxurious and extravagant suite.”

Prefer:
“This suite offers additional space and privacy, along with a private terrace overlooking the courtyard.”

### 6.2 Example: Responding to a Late Check-Out Request

Rather than:
“Unfortunately that’s not possible.”

Prefer:
“We would be happy to explore options for a later departure. Let me check availability and come back to you shortly.”

These examples illustrate tone and phrasing. They do not create new mandatory standards beyond those defined above.

---

## 7. Internal Note (Not for Public Reference)

As we continue expanding into new markets, there may be commercial pressure to adopt stronger luxury positioning language. While this may increase short-term bookings, it risks diluting the brand long-term. Any significant shift in positioning must be approved by the Brand Director.

This note is for internal strategic awareness and does not amend the standards above.
